Travel Tips

Hard-earned advice from 8 years on the road — so you can travel smarter, safer, and cheaper.

Pack Light, Travel Right

Stick to a carry-on whenever possible. Rolling clothes saves space, packing cubes keep you organised, and a lighter bag means more freedom and fewer fees.

Always Get Travel Insurance

One medical emergency abroad can cost tens of thousands. A good travel policy is the best $50 you'll spend. Don't skip it — ever.

Learn Basic Local Phrases

"Hello", "Thank you", and "Where is the toilet?" in the local language opens doors, earns respect, and leads to the best off-menu food.

Use a No-Fee Travel Card

Avoid airport currency kiosks. Cards like Wise or Revolut give you near-interbank exchange rates and save you hundreds over a long trip.

Download Offline Maps

Google Maps offline or Maps.me are lifesavers. Download the region before you land so you never need expensive roaming data to navigate.

Visit Attractions Early

The magic hour before 9 AM gives you iconic spots almost to yourself. Sunrise at Angkor Wat or the Eiffel Tower with no crowds is a completely different experience.
